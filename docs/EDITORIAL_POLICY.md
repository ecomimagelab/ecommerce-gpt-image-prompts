# Editorial and Quality Policy

The library optimizes for usefulness and reproducibility, not raw record count.

## In scope

- product main images;
- white-background catalog images;
- lifestyle scenes;
- detail pages and A+ modules;
- feature diagrams and exploded views;
- packaging and brand concepts;
- marketplace ads and social commerce;
- virtual try-on and model workflows;
- food menus and delivery listings;
- multilingual e-commerce graphics.

## Out of scope

- prompts unrelated to commerce;
- unsafe or sexually explicit content;
- deceptive before-and-after claims;
- unlicensed impersonation or likeness use;
- counterfeit or fraudulent listings;
- copied prompts without attribution;
- inaccessible or unrelated preview images;
- prompts that instruct users to evade platform policies or laws.

## Quality dimensions

Maintainers evaluate:

1. **Reproducibility** — the prompt is complete enough to run.
2. **Commercial usefulness** — the output solves a recognizable commerce task.
3. **Product consistency** — constraints protect geometry, packaging, color,
   text, and identity where relevant.
4. **Preview fidelity** — previews actually demonstrate the prompt.
5. **Metadata quality** — industry, use case, platform, source, and license are accurate.
6. **Safety and rights** — publication is reasonably safe and supportable.

## Collection policy

Public availability does not automatically authorize republication. Content
discovered on the open web must not be added until its author, source, and
permission basis are recorded. When rights are unclear, link to the source for
review rather than publishing the prompt or image.
