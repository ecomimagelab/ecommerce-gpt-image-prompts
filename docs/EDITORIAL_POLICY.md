# Editorial and Quality Policy

The library optimizes for production usefulness, visual quality, product trust,
and reproducibility rather than raw record count.

## In scope

- marketplace main and catalog images;
- product-edit background, lighting, and composition workflows;
- lifestyle scenes, detail images, feature visuals, and A+ modules;
- packaging concepts and brand heroes;
- marketplace ads and social-commerce creative;
- on-model and virtual-try-on workflows with appropriate consent;
- food menus, delivery listings, and global storefront localization;
- lawful Before/After demonstrations.

## Out of scope

- prompts unrelated to commerce;
- unsafe or sexually explicit content;
- counterfeit, deceptive, or policy-evasion workflows;
- unlicensed impersonation, likeness, characters, artwork, or source photography;
- copied prompts or preview images without compatible permission;
- unrelated or inaccessible preview images;
- unsupported product, medical, environmental, safety, or performance claims;
- guaranteed platform approval or guaranteed generative-model fidelity;
- Pinterest assets submitted on attribution alone.

## Review dimensions

1. **Single-image reproducibility** — every variant can be run independently.
2. **Commercial usefulness** — the output solves a recognizable seller task.
3. **Product fidelity** — geometry, packaging, material, color, label, and included
   parts are protected where relevant.
4. **Visual quality** — composition, hierarchy, lighting, material rendering, and
   mobile-thumbnail clarity meet the authoring standard.
5. **Preview fidelity** — examples demonstrate the recorded prompt and inputs.
6. **Taxonomy accuracy** — category, use case, background, style, purpose, and
   platform IDs are valid and useful.
7. **Claims discipline** — all factual copy and demonstrations are supplied and
   verified by the merchant.
8. **Rights and provenance** — every redistributed asset has a documented basis.
9. **Platform precision** — design guidance is clearly separated from claims
   based on current official policy sources.
10. **Localization honesty** — missing content translations visibly fall back to
    the source language.

## Visual research

Public web sources may guide broad, non-exclusive art-direction choices. A
research link is not a source image and is not a license. Reviewers compare
submissions for overly close replication of distinctive works and may request a
new composition or generated example.

## Approval stages

1. A community Issue receives editorial, safety, metadata, and rights review.
2. `needs-changes`, `rights-review`, `visual-qa`, or `platform-review` may be used.
3. `approved` means the Issue passed the first review.
4. Automation creates a structured-data pull request.
5. A maintainer performs final review of the generated JSON, examples, links,
   README rendering, and validation results.
6. Publication occurs only when the pull request is merged.
