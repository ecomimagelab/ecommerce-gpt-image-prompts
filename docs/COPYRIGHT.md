# Copyright, Attribution, and Takedown Policy

This repository is a public index of prompts, metadata, and example outputs.
It is not a grant of rights to third-party brands, trademarks, packaging,
characters, people, source photographs, or other protected material.

## Repository-authored material

Unless stated otherwise, original prompt text, structured records,
documentation, translations, and repository-authored preview images are
licensed under CC BY 4.0.

When reusing repository-authored material:

1. credit the named author or “CommercePrompt Atlas contributors”;
2. link to this repository or the relevant record;
3. link to CC BY 4.0;
4. state whether you modified the material.

## Community material

Community records include author, source, license, and review metadata. The
repository’s CC BY 4.0 license applies only to material the contributor had the
right to license. It does not erase or replace third-party rights.

## Trademarks and commercial use

Platform and brand names may appear descriptively to identify an e-commerce
workflow. Their owners do not sponsor or endorse this repository. Before using
a prompt commercially, users must review applicable trademark, advertising,
consumer-protection, privacy, publicity, and intellectual-property rules.

## Reporting a problem

Use the **Copyright or rights report** Issue form to request:

- removal;
- corrected attribution;
- corrected license metadata;
- removal of a personal likeness or private information;
- review of trademark or publicity-right concerns.

Include the prompt ID or repository URL, your relationship to the work, the
requested resolution, and non-sensitive supporting evidence.

Do not publish identity documents, private addresses, private contracts, or
other sensitive evidence in a public Issue. A maintainer may provide a private
contact channel when necessary.

## Review process

Maintainers may temporarily mark disputed content as `removed` while reviewing
a report. Valid corrections and removal requests should be handled promptly.
The project may remove material at its discretion even when a legal
determination has not been made.

This policy is an operational policy, not legal advice and not a substitute for
a formal legal notice where one is required.
